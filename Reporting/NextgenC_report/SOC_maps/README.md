# Gridded soil organic carbon (SOC) maps of Finland — HIKET / NextGenC

Kriged maps of topsoil/total soil organic carbon stock for Finnish forest land,
1985–2024, from six decomposition models and their ensemble. Produced by the
HIKET Bayesian model-intercomparison project for the NextGenC reporting effort.

**This is a demonstration / illustrative product.** See *Interpretation &
limitations* below before using the maps quantitatively.

---

## Files

For each model `M` ∈ {SP1, TP2, TP3, Yasso07, Yasso15, Yasso20}, plus the
six-model `ENSEMBLE`:

| File | Description |
|---|---|
| `M_SOC_mean.tif` | Mean SOC stock (tC ha⁻¹), 40 bands = years 1985–2024 |
| `M_SOC_sd.tif`   | Total uncertainty (1 SD, tC ha⁻¹), 40 bands, same grid/years |

14 GeoTIFFs total. In each file, **band _n_ = year 1984 + _n_** (band 1 = 1985 …
band 40 = 2024); the year is also stored as the band/layer name (`y1985` …).
Band _n_ of a `_sd` file is the uncertainty of band _n_ of the matching `_mean`
file (identical grid, year, and land mask).

### Point-level SOC value matrices (accompanying tabular deliverable)

The same posterior-predictive SOC that the maps interpolate is also provided
**per plot**, as plain tables — the underlying numbers behind the maps:

| File | Description |
|---|---|
| `<MODEL>_SOC_mean.csv` | Mean SOC (tC ha⁻¹), rows = 447 plots, cols = years 1985–2024 |
| `<MODEL>_SOC_sd.csv`   | Posterior SD (tC ha⁻¹), same shape |
| `Finland_SOC_matrices.ods` | All 12 matrices as one workbook (one sheet per model × {mean, sd}) |

12 CSVs + 1 ODS (6 models × 2). Generated by `../build_soc_matrices.R`. These
are the **measurement-grid values**; the GeoTIFFs are their kriged
interpolation. Note `_sd` here is the *parameter* spread on the predictive mean
(observation error not added).

### Ensemble SOC change (delta) map

`ENSEMBLE_SOC_delta10yr.tif` — single-band annual SOC change (**tC ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹**)
over the last decade (**2015–2024**), as the per-cell OLS slope of the ensemble
mean across those 10 years. Positive = SOC gain, negative = loss (~76% of cells
gain, ~24% lose; mean +0.13). Generated by `../make_delta_map.R`.

### Thumbnails

`thumbnails/` holds one PNG per raster (`<MODEL>_SOC_mean.png`, `_sd.png`,
`ENSEMBLE_*`, and `ENSEMBLE_SOC_delta10yr.png`), each the **1985–2024
time-average** of that layer (the delta is the 2015–2024 change). ~2000 px tall,
300 dpi. Generated by `../make_thumbnails.R` (+ `../make_delta_map.R`). Palettes:
mean = YlGnBu, SD = Purples (calm), delta = diverging red(loss)→blue(gain) centred
on zero. In all thumbnails **peat = black, water/sea = white**.

## How the maps were made

1. **Source.** Posterior-predictive SOC at ~447 Finnish forest inventory plots
   (NFI / Biosoil / MUSTIKKA / Komeetta), from each model's Bayesian
   calibration. Each plot carries a posterior-predictive **mean** and **SD** per
   year.
2. **Interpolation.** Ordinary kriging in **log space** (SOC is positive and
   right-skewed), back-transformed to tC ha⁻¹. One pooled variogram per model
   (fitted on the per-plot temporal-mean log field), reused across years.
3. **Per-model uncertainty (`_sd`).** Combines, in log space, the **kriging
   (interpolation) variance** — which grows away from plots — with the
   **interpolated model posterior uncertainty**, then back-transformed
   (lognormal SD).
4. **Peat & water exclusion.** The maps target **mineral-soil forest SOC**.
   Peat/water calibration plots are dropped from the interpolation input, and grid
   cells are masked to NoData where they are **>50% peatland** (Luke MS-NFI
   `paatyyppi`, 16 m; spruce/pine mire + treeless peatland) or **>50% water**
   (SYKE Ranta10 lakes; the sea is outside the land polygon). In the **thumbnails**,
   peat renders **black** and water/sea render **white**; in the GeoTIFFs both are
   simply NoData.
5. **Ensemble.** Per-model surfaces are combined on the grid, equal-weighted.
   The ensemble SD applies the law of total variance:
   `sd² = mean_i(sd_i²)  +  var_i(mean_i)`,
   i.e. **within-model uncertainty + between-model (structural) spread**. Where
   the between-model term dominates, the maps are uncertain because the models
   *disagree*, not because the data are sparse.

Reproduce with `../build_soc_maps.R` (R; requires `terra`, `gstat`, `sf`,
`sp`). Methods note: `../SOC_maps_README.pdf`.

## Interpretation & limitations

- **Spatial detail is limited by the plot network.** Plots are ~27 km apart on
  average, and SOC is spatially weakly autocorrelated at that spacing (the
  variogram is ~85–90 % nugget). The maps therefore reproduce the **national
  mean field with plot-anchored local deviations**; smooth sub-regional gradients
  are *not* resolvable from these points. Detail between plots is interpolation,
  not measurement.
- **Read the `_sd` layer.** It is the honest statement of confidence — it inflates
  between plots and in data-sparse regions (e.g. northern Lapland). High SD =
  "the data cannot see here."
- **Point SOC predictability is intrinsically low** in this system (calibration
  R² ≈ 0.05–0.11); the weak spatial structure is the geographic echo of the same
  limit. Use the maps for illustration and relative model/ensemble comparison,
  not as a fine-scale SOC inventory.
- **The mean surface is a back-transformed log-kriging (median) estimate**; in
  data-sparse areas it reverts toward the national mean.

## Provenance & versioning

- Generated by HIKET `build_soc_maps.R`, `set.seed(2025)`.
- Model posterior RUN_IDs are recorded in the build script (`bundles`).
- **TP3 caveat (this release):** the TP3 maps use the pre-fix (forward-Euler)
  posterior; the exact matrix-exponential re-calibration supersedes it. SOC
  levels change negligibly. Replace the TP3 layers when the updated posterior is
  available.
